Mobile App: Likely - Travel Planner

"Likely" was drafted in anticipation of the easing of global travel restrictions - with tourism bouncing back to complete normalcy. The mobile app would give its users the ability to plan their trips from beginning to end, with all activities, sights, tours and transit options - as well as personalised recommendations - available at the user's convenience. Activities are bookmarked, reordered and reallocated in chunks, meaning that the user has complete control of their itinerary, and can adjust or readjust their plans to their heart's content, before making any confirmations.

Team Size, Role & Responsibilities

The project was completed at an individual level, but conversations regarding user research were shared amongst others in the class to achieve a comprehensive understanding of what is otherwise an intensely complicated and intricate geopolitical negotiation.

Deliverables

A formal presentation and slide deck (found below) comprising of all research artefacts and a demonstration of the application prototype - with time alloted for questions at the end.

Tools Used

Google Drive, Slides, Docs, Sheets, Miro, Figma and Figjam.


Design Process


Interviews with previously-avid travellers were planned with the aim of understanding the usual and not-so-usual considerations people take into account before travelling - and the impact that covid-19 had had on their intentions to travel again. 6 interviewees were asked 18 questions, and their answers formed the basis for an Affinity Diagram, User Persona (Herb Lemmon), Empathy Map. These insights kickstarted the ideation phase of the project.

With the conclusion of an in-depth user research process, a user insight statement was solidified and a problem statement finalised.

Began with an "I Like, I Wish, What If" Brainstorm session amongst peers. Insights were shared and a myriad of potential project directions were generated. From here, a Feature Prioritisation Matrix was drafted to help decide which features of a travel planner app would be best placed and most appreciated by users, given the time and resource constraints of the project. With a clearer goal in mind, a Value Proposition Statement was formalised and a storyboard/User Journey Map was produced to communicate the aims and goals of the application.

A number of sketch pages were produced, with the aim of exploring questions around button placement, mapping, iconography, card content and chunking, and even, some heuristic development. These sketches were translated into a high-fidelity Figma mockup, which remained truthful to the user flow identified in the ideation phase. Wireframes were linked together to create an illustrative prototype, ready for user testing and feedback.
